Biomedical Signal Monitoring

 A biometric signal monitoring platform is a system that allows continuous monitoring and recording of biometric signals. In this research, we delve into sensors, wearable devices, data collection software, and algorithms. Through the utilization of mobile and wearable systems, signals like heart rate, muscle activity, brainwaves, and movement data are collected. These signals are then monitored in real-time via smartphones or computers. By consistently tracking one's health status, the potential for early disease detection and treatment is heightened. The utilization of remote healthcare further facilitates the observation of a patient's condition from a distance. Real-time monitoring of numerical changes allows for the establishment of personalized diagnosis and treatment plans tailored to individuals.

Biomedical Signal Processing & Analysis

 Biomedical signal processing and analysis involve understanding signals generated within the human body and their meanings. This includes signal collection, noise removal, extraction of crucial features, specific pattern recognition, and deriving indicators for disease diagnosis. Processed biometric signals contribute to understanding the human condition and assist physicians in diagnosing diseases, understanding treatment progress, and making informed decisions. Biomedical signal processing finds widespread utilization in fields such as cardiology, neurology, and sleep research. It is a key technology for non-invasively observing health status, detecting issues early, and providing tailored treatments without invasive methods.

Cardiovascular Health Assessment

 This topic primarily emphasizes predicting the risk of cardiovascular diseases through various factors and indicators, and proposing methods for prevention along with personalized medical plans for individuals. In this research, measurements and analysis are conducted on various aspects, including electrocardiography (ECG) for the heart's electrical signals, photoplethysmography (PPG) for assessing blood volume changes, and analyzing blood pressure waveforms. These evaluations provide insights into the cardiovascular health status. The study also involves utilizing demographic data, clinical information, and even detailed genetic profiles to estimate an individual's potential risk of cardiovascular diseases. Furthermore, this research includes the prediction and evaluation of cardiac activity through digital twin-based virtual model simulations.

Clinical Decision Support

 The most common tests performed in modern medicine are clinical chemistry tests such as blood tests and urine tests. These tests are conducted frequently at hospitals and health screening centers on a daily basis. However, there are numerous factors that can introduce errors into the testing process, and these errors can sometimes lead to critical outcomes once the test results are conveyed to the physicians. In this research, various statistical and artificial intelligence techniques are utilized to reduce errors in test results. Additionally, effective automated verification technology is developed to streamline the manual error verification process in cases where errors occur.

Digital Medicine

 Digital Medicine encompasses the integration of technology, including artificial intelligence (AI), data analytics, and the Internet of Things (IoT), into healthcare. It leverages patient-generated health data collected through wearable devices and IoT sensors, harnessing AI for data analysis and predictive modeling. This convergence of technology enables personalized healthcare, enhances diagnostic accuracy, and facilitates more efficient healthcare delivery, ultimately improving patient outcomes and treatment approaches.
