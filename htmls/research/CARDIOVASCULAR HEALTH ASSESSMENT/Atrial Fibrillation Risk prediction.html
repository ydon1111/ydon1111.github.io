Cardiovascular Health Assessment

AF prediction

Importance of early diagnosis of Atrial Fibrillation continues to grow, but without dignositic and prognostic technologies, approaching to recovery/cure is challenging. Integration of genome-wide association studies(GWAS) and clinical information is used to develop predictive model, which increases the early detection rate of atrial fibrillation and enables the prediction of its prognosis. Discovering specific genetic markers related to the disease through artificial intelligence(AI), which may not be identified using conventional statistical methods, will improve individual's prediction of potential risk of atrial fibrillation based on their genetic characteristics. Our research aims to improve the accuracy of predicting atrial fibrillation to a clinical level and expects to be an expanded use of a diagnositc system for other diseases. Using genetic data as the component of the fusion of heterogeneous data, we expect the development of high-precision diagnostic technology.

Predicting Potential Pathological Risks Using Machine Learning-Based Genomic Data Analysis

Utilizing Machine Learning on Genomic Data for Predicting Disease Outcomes: Shifting from Previous Emphasis on Predicting Height and BMI, to Recent Focus on Diseases Requiring Further Investigation due to Unclear Genetic Predisposition (Beyond Cardiovascular Diseases). In the realm of Deep Learning, recent advancements have seen the utilization of CNNs to predict phenotypes from image data. Subsequently, GWAS analysis is conducted using phenotypes extracted from image data to unveil associated genetic factors


Recurrence AF prediction based on Heterogeneous data

In the project focusing on the diagnosis and prognosis of Atrial Fibrillation (AF), genetic and clinical information is being utilized to discern atrial fibrillation patterns. The project aims to analyze and predict the diagnosis and prognosis of atrial fibrillation, utilizing atrial geometry and electrophysiological data.
