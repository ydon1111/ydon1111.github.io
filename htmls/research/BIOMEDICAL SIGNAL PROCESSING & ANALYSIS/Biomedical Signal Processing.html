Biomedical Signal Processing

Biomedical signal processing is a specialized field in biomedical engineering and healthcare that focuses on the acquisition, analysis, and interpretation of physiological signals generated by the human body. These signals, which can include electrocardiograms (ECG), electroencephalograms (EEG), and various imaging data, provide crucial information about a person's health and well-being. Biomedical signal processing involves the application of advanced mathematical and computational techniques to extract relevant information from these signals, such as diagnosing medical conditions, monitoring vital signs, and assessing treatment efficacy. This field plays a pivotal role in modern healthcare, aiding in the early detection of diseases, guiding medical interventions, and enhancing our understanding of complex physiological processes.
