Research Topics

CLINICAL DECISION SUPPORT

Pain assessment

The goal of this research is to quantitatively measure the subjective experience of pain for individuals using signals emitted by our bodies. In this research, technology is developed to monitor pain in individuals who have difficulty expressing their pain, such as patients under anesthesia during surgery. If an accurate method of measuring pain can be established, physicians can administer appropriate amounts of anesthesia or pain-relieving medications during surgery. Furthermore, post-surgery, unnecessary medication usage can be curtailed, potentially improving patient outcomes.

Observation of Heart Rate Changes After Endotracheal Intubation

The purpose of this item description is to quantitatively assess a patient's acute pain through HR (Heart Rate) measurements. Furthermore, it aims to utilize the patient's HR values for early prediction of pain, enabling personalized treatment strategies to be implemented.


Recurrence AF prediction based on Heterogeneous data

In the project focusing on the diagnosis and prognosis of Atrial Fibrillation (AF), genetic and clinical information is being utilized to discern atrial fibrillation patterns. The project aims to analyze and predict the diagnosis and prognosis of atrial fibrillation, utilizing atrial geometry and electrophysiological data.
